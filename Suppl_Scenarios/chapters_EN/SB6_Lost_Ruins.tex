
\newgamingscenario{1}{4}{Lost Ruins}
\label{LostRuins}

\flufffont{Mist shrouds these long forgotten ruins, though somewhere a treasure is hidden. You cannot risk sending your entire force in at once, going forward without a vanguard would be foolish.}

\subsection*{Deployment}

Deployment Zones are areas more than \distance{14} away from the Centre Line.

\subsection*{Pre-Game Set-up}

During step 6 of the Pre-Game Sequence, immediately after it has been determined which player will be choosing \rewordedrule{their} Deployment Zone but before the choice has been made, the player not choosing \rewordedrule{their} Deployment Zone must choose a \rewordedrule{single} Terrain Feature on the \rewordedrule{Battlefield}. This Terrain Feature is known as the Lost \rewordedrule{Vault}.

When declaring Special Deployment, during step 8 of the Pre-\rewordedrule{G}ame Sequence, both players \rewordedrule{alternately} choose \rewordedrule{two} of their units \rewordedrule{each} to gain \textbf{Vanguard}\rewordedrule{, starting with the player that chose their Deployment Zone}. \rewordedrule{Lone} Characters cannot be chosen.

\subsection*{Winning the Secondary Objective}

At the end of the game, the player with the most Scoring Units within \distance{6} of the Lost Ruins wins th\rewordedrule{is} Secondary Objective.
